29.

Milivojevic v Raiffeisenbank St. Stefan-Jagerberg-Wolfsberg eGen

 

20/02/2019

 

Cases

 

 

European Union Freedom to provide services. Article 17(1) of Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012 had to be interpreted as meaning that a debtor who had entered into a credit agreement in order to have renovation work carried out in an immovable property which was his domicile with the intention, in particular, of providing tourist accommodation services could not be regarded as a 'consumer' within the meaning of that provision, unless, in the light of the context of the transaction, regarded as a whole, for which the contract had been concluded, that contract had such a tenuous link to that professional activity that it appeared clear that the contract was essentially for private purposes, which was a matter for the referring court to ascertain. The Court of Justice of the European Union so held, among other things, in a preliminary ruling in proceedings between the parties concerning an action for a declaration of invalidity of a credit agreement and of a notarised deed related to the creation of a mortgage taken out to guarantee the debt arising out of that contract and the removal of that security from the land register.

Appeal Tracker: United Company Rusal plc (a company incorporated in Jersey) v Crispian Investments Ltd (a company incorporated in Cyprus) and another company

 

20/02/2019

 

Cases

 

 

Appeal Tracker Alert: Hearing of appeal in the Court of Appeal to be heard by 18 November 2019. Appeal from the order of Honourable Justice Phillips HCJ Queens Bench Division Commercial Court dated 27 November 2018. In the previous judgment, the first defendant shareholder's notice to two other shareholders of a company (the claimant and the second defendant), offering to sell a percentage of the company's share capital, and purporting to commence a 'right of first refusal' procedure pursuant to a shareholders' agreement, was invalid and ineffective. Accordingly, the Commercial Court, having interpreted the shareholders' agreement according to usual principles applicable to commercial contracts, ruled that the first defendant was precluded under the terms of that agreement from disposing of shares in the way it had sought to.

This enactment gives effect to specific provisions in customs arrangements between the UK and Guernsey in order to maintain the current customs relationship after the UK leaves the EU. Guernsey has a distinct customs relationship with the UK, which arises from the terms of the UKs accession to the EU and allows goods to be imported into the UK from Guernsey free from customs duty.
